When identifying key terms and phrases related to a research topic, extract not only full terms but also relevant sub-terms and variants.
For example, if the topic is “software engineering,” include:

the full phrase (“software engineering”),

its components (“software”, “engineering”),

synonyms or related terms (“software design”, “system engineering”, etc.),

spelling variants and abbreviations (e.g., “SW engineering”),

hyphenated forms and variations (e.g., “software-engineering”, “engineering-driven”),

and grammatical variations (e.g., “engineers”, “engineering”, “engineered”).
